Santiago Cadavid

Biomedical Engineer

I am a Biomedical Engineer with experience measuring, acquiring and processing physiological human signals as well as controlling environmental variables in hospitals and clinical laboratories. I have a good understanding of a wide range of medical devices , electronics principles and medical concept which allow me to liaise between the clinical personnel and engineers making me an effective member in cross-functional teams. On top of my technical background, I have a general understanding of ICT management and digital transformation at a corporate level with emphasis on innovation and marketing due to my recent studies in Technology Management.


A bit about me is that I enjoy learning the working principle of everything, the reason why I like to read about physics and science. In my free time, I like to write code, build electronics projects and print my own designs on my 3D printer. I like zombie video games, swimming and cooking.

I did my bachelor degree in Biomedical Engineering at the Metropolitan Institute of Technology in Medellín, Colombia. During my undergraduate study, I had the awesome opportunity to teach two courses: Electromagnetic Physics and Bioinstrumentation. At that time, I discovered I had a huge passion and an innate talent for teaching.


Also, I developed a wide variety of projects for different subjects like a myoelectric controlled prosthesis (Bioinstrumentation), an electric wheelchair tongue-based control for quadriplegia patients (Rehabilitation Engineering), an ECG wave simulator software (Sensors), a neonatal incubator simulator device (Medical Device Ddesign), etc.

During my last undergraduate year, I was part of Gi2B, developing a research project sponsored by COLCIENCIAS whose aim was to design and create a diagnostic software for detection of swallowing disorders using sEMG and Machine Learning. 

To know more about Gi2B Research Group click here.

Right now I am working in the Medical Device industry as a Biomedical Engineer , performing preventive/scheduled maintenance on medical devices, and installing, calibrating, repairing and inspecting medical equipment in medical centers around New Zealand.


Previosly, I worked as a Field Service Engineer for a company where my key responsabilities were to design, build and install telemetric sensors systems for hospitals and clinical laboratories. Consequently, I had to be in contact with the engineer teams of different hospitals around the metropolitan area, which helped me out to develop personal relationships and communication skills.


During the Pandemic situation caused by Covid-19 in 2020, I collaborated with a small non profit organization, where a cross functional team of talent professionals were developing a low-cost prototype of mechanical ventilation system.
