Santiago Cadavid

Biomedical Engineer

I am a Biomedical Engineer with experience measuring, acquiring and processing human physiological signals, controlling environmental variables in hospital sensors and installing, calibrating, repairing and inspecting medical equipment. I have a good understanding of a wide range of medical devices, electronics principles and medical concepts, which allow me to liaise between the clinical personnel and engineers making me an influential member of cross-functional teams. On top of my technical background, I have a general understanding of ICT management and digital transformation at a corporate level with an emphasis on innovation and marketing due to my recent studies in Technology Management.


A bit about me is that I enjoy learning the working principle of everything, which is why I like to read about physics and science. I enjoy writing code to build electronics projects and printing my designs on my 3D printer in my free time.

I did my bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering at the Metropolitan Institute of Technology in Medellín, Colombia. During my undergraduate study, I had the incredible opportunity to teach two courses: Electromagnetic Physics and Bioinstrumentation. At that time, I discovered I had a huge passion and an innate talent for teaching.


Also, I developed a wide variety of projects for different subjects like a myoelectric controlled prosthesis (Bioinstrumentation paper), an electric wheelchair tongue-based control for quadriplegia patients (Rehabilitation Engineering paper), an ECG wave simulator software (Sensors paper), a neonatal incubator simulator device (Medical Device Design paper), etc.

During my last undergraduate year, I was part of Gi2B, developing a research project sponsored by COLCIENCIAS whose aim was to design and create diagnostic software for the detection of swallowing disorders using sEMG and Machine Learning. 

To know more about Gi2B Research Group click here.

I am currently working in the Medical Device industry as a Biomedical Engineer, performing preventive/scheduled maintenance on medical devices and installing, calibrating, repairing and inspecting medical equipment in medical centres around New Zealand.


Previously, I worked as a Field Service Engineer for a company where my key responsibilities were designing, building and installing telemetric sensor systems for hospitals and clinical laboratories. Consequently, I had to contact the engineer teams of different hospitals around the metropolitan area, which helped me develop personal relationships and communication skills.


During the pandemic caused by Covid-19 in 2020, I collaborated with a small non-profit organization, where a cross-functional team of talented professionals aimed to develop a low-cost prototype of a mechanical ventilation system.
